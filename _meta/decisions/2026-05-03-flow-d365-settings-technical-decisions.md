# 2026-05-03 — Flow / D365 / Settings / Technical Decisions

Source: live product-owner decisions from 2026-05-03 Hermes session.

## Canonical NPD flow

Canonical MVP flow:

1. Brief creates an NPD project, e.g. `DEV-123`.
2. The project moves through Stage-Gate G0-G4.
3. At G3 the project creates or maps the Finished Good (FG) candidate.
4. All required departments close their sections and provide the minimum data required to create product, specification, BOM and financial records.
5. After all departments close and the project is approved, NPD Builder generates:
   - all WIP/intermediate items needed to manufacture the product,
   - the FG product,
   - the validated BOM/product records.
6. From that point, FG and WIP items are available to the factory, Planning and Technical.
7. After release, Technical owns factory specification maintenance and checks/updates specification correctness when meats/raw materials, allergens or BOM details change.

Stage-Gate G0-G4 is MVP scope.

The legacy-looking Trial / Pilot / Handoff / Packaging stages are not deprecated; they return as part of NPD and must be represented in the NPD flow.

Sensory belongs under Technical, not as an independent NPD sensory module.

## Canonical naming

`FG` / Finished Good is the accepted canonical product term for the released sellable product in UI, PRD, tasks and production domain language.

Legacy `FA` / Factory Article terminology may appear only as a migration/compatibility alias and must not be used as the final user-facing domain term.

## BOM SSOT and release model

The best target architecture is one shared BOM table/model as the single source of truth across NPD Builder, Technical, Planning, Production and integrations.

NPD Builder creates the initial BOM version together with WIP/intermediate items and the FG. After release, the shared BOM remains the source of truth. D365 import/export can sync with it but cannot supersede it.

## NPD edit-after-release policy

After NPD Builder creates the WIP/FG/product/BOM records, NPD may still request edits to released product/BOM data only when authorized. Authorization is configurable per organization.

A released BOM/product edit must create a new BOM/product-spec version rather than mutating the currently approved factory version in-place. The new version then goes to Technical for physical specification review and approval before it becomes approved for factory use.

## D365 posture

D365 is an integration only, not the superior/canonical system.

Monopilot is an independent creator and system of record for product, BOM, WIP/FG and factory-facing master data.

D365 capabilities are limited to integration convenience:

- easy sending/exporting of products, BOMs and orders from Monopilot to D365;
- easy import/export of BOMs and products between Monopilot and D365;
- Excel-row style transfer/export is acceptable for the current phase;
- no product strategy, flow or canonical data model should depend on D365;
- do not cache D365 data as a strategic dependency;
- D365-specific behavior exists only if the organization enables that integration level.

D365 item/BOM/product export/publish is required, but must be modelled as optional integration/export from Monopilot-owned data. Overwrite/import from D365 can exist only through an authorized person / authorized workflow.

## Technical ownership

FG becomes available to the factory/Planning/Technical when all departments close, the project is approved, and NPD Builder has generated/validated product/BOM/WIP records.

WIP/intermediate items are generated by NPD Builder.

Supplier specs are Phase 1.

Lab results are Quality-owned; Technical may consume/read them for specification and factory correctness workflows.

Spec taxonomy decision:

- supplier_spec: supplier-provided raw material/component parameters, certificates, allergens, cost/quality constraints and approved supplier facts;
- customer_spec: the outgoing product specification promised to a customer/private-label/market, including packaging, label, claims, nutritional/compliance and product parameters;
- factory_spec / internal_product_spec: the manufacturing-facing specification used by Technical/factory for BOM, process, allergens, shelf-life, print specs and QC limits.

`factory_spec` / `internal_product_spec` is the accepted Phase 1 Technical-owned specification concept for factory use. Customer-facing specification may exist as a separate derived/customer-facing artifact, but factory use must rely on the Technical-approved factory_spec/internal_product_spec.

## PO / TO / RM usability decisions

Purchase Orders must not silently update approved supplier_spec or BOM cost_per_kg. PO records actual purchase price, supplier lot, supplier item and delivery facts. Material or price deviations can trigger review/non-conformance events for supplier_spec, item cost history, allergen/spec review or finance approval.

Transfer Orders must not change Technical master specification. TO may affect inventory availability, site/location, lot genealogy and shelf-life remaining; specification changes only through explicit spec/BOM/change-control workflows. If TO reveals an issue, it triggers non-conformance/review rather than mutating spec.

Technical/BOM must validate raw material/component usability before BOM use. Validation examples: approved supplier, active supplier_spec, allergen profile compatible with product, item active, cost/spec review not blocked, required QC/release status present where applicable.
